RAL Housing & End Covers

Housing

  • Housing is normally of Cast construction for rigidity & cost.
  • Fabrications are used for non standard configurations & 'one-off' large valves
  • Inlet Types:
    • Inline Inlets:
      • Classic configuration - practical used for most
    • Offset Inlets
      • To ensure valve never achieve 100% filling
      • To minimise incident of product shearing

End Covers

  • Categorized into two parts:
    • With inboardmounted bearings:
      • Low cost, Low duty valves
      • Offered for low pressure differential applications
      • Filter / cyclone & general discharge duties, where he product does not exhibit difficult handling characteristics
    • With bearings outboard mounted / on trigger extensions:
      • To protect bearing from direct product contamination
      • To provide space for accomodating a wide range of alternate shaft seasling arrangements
      • They are inherently largeer to accomodate larger rotor shaft diameters necessary to limit rotor deflections under high operating loads
  • Air purging can be included:
    • To dislodge products at valve outlet that tend to hang in rotor pockets
    • To pressurise the void that exists between End Cover & End Discs when using Closed Rotors to prevent product entry

Blowing Seals or Blow Through Models

  • Direct connection into Pneumatic Conveying Blowlines
  • Direct Purge:
    • True Blow through of the Rotor Pocket
    • Most efficient & covers wider range of materials
    • Restricts the drive arrangements that can be fitted due to closeness of pipe connections to the Rotor Shaft
  • Semi Direct Purge:
    • Have an Entrainment Trough
    • Conveying line Passes partlyalong the trough & partly through the Rotor
    • More commmon

Entrainment Boxes

  • For applications where blow through is not suitable
  • Normal Drop Through Rotary valves are used with separate Transition box to connect to Blow line
  • Common & can be adapted for different pipe sizes - prefered for some products & conditions anyway
  • Straight sided designs tapering down to the pipe are effective for most applications & the deeper the better.
