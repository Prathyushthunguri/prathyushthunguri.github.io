RAL Selection Criteria

Basic Performance Requirement:-

  • Key Factor - Rotor Speed
  • Normally Accepted - Rotor Tip Speed - 40 meters/min (Max) - above this incease in speed decreases throughput
  • Explanation:
    • When the rotary valve is gravity-fed (material simply falls into it), the product must:
      • Accelerate from rest
      • Fill rotor pockets before they rotate away from the inlet.
    • But if the rotor tip is moving too fast:
      • The product doesn’t have enough time to fill the pockets.
      • This leads to incomplete pocket filling, reducing the actual amount of material delivered per rotation.
      • Hence, total throughput drops, even though the speed is higher.
  • Value may be lower if:
    • Product has low bulk density
      • Light materials take longer to settle.
      • At higher rotor speeds, these materials won’t fill pockets fully before being carried away.
    • Significant pressure differential giving rise to airflow opposing product flow
      • Upward airflow can push product back, reducing the filling efficiency.
      • Cause aeration (fluidization), making the material behave like a fluid, which worsens filling at higher speeds.
  • Value may be higher if:
    • Fed under cascade flow conditions such as screw feeders, belt feeders & weighers
      • Material enters rotor pockets more consistently and forcefully.
      • Rotor doesn’t need to "wait" for the material to fall in.
      • Higher tip speeds can be used without losing throughput.
  • Considering throat size - it is difficult to achieve good results at smaller sizes with high speeds even though it falls below normally accepted Tip speed.
  • Normal Practice:
    • For valve with rotor diameter upto 300 mm - select that gives rotor speeds below 25 RPM
    • Above 300 mm - go below 40 m/min tip speed

Product Physical Characteristics:-

  • For materials that become abrasive when entrained by high velocity leakage air - causing erosion
    • Lower the Pressure Differential is better

System / Duty Requirements:-

  • Physical Installation Details
    • Flange Details: Square or Round or Combination
    • Type of drive: chain or direct coupled
    • Inbuilt venting present or not
  • Pressure Differential
    • Higher Pressure differential - greater leakage of air
    • High upward leakage causes:
      • Reduced material flow by reducing bulk density through fluidisation
      • Physically opposing flow
      • Enhance abrasiveness of hard fine particles
      • Transfer moisture & create unstable flow conditions
  • Operating Clearances & Temperature
    • For minimal leakage under ambient temperature condittions - nominal clearance is:
      • Cast Iron / Carbon Steel - 0.13 mm
      • Stainless Steel - 0.18 mm - To minimise the risk of galling / siezing should the Rotor make contact with the Housing under extreme load conditions or due to bearing failure etc.
    • Where the operating temperature exceeds ambient by more than 30 deg C then clearances start to need to be increased.
    • Rotor & Housing expand at different rates with temperature fluctuations
      • Rotor is lighter component and is not subject to surface heat loss unlike the Housing
