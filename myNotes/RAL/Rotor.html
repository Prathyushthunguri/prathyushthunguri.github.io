RAL Rotor

Rotor Design & Types

  • Two fundamental types:
    • Open Vane
      • Performs better - adaptable with special features - more tolerant to changes in product - can be purged if necessary
    • Closed Vane
      • Not suitable for use in Blowing Seals
      • Problems include:
        • Product ingressing into the void between End Cover & Rotor disc
        • High wear in he Bore where there is close proximity with the Rotor Disc & increased risk of product 'hang-up' in the pockets.
      • Pros:
        • Gives the Vanes more support & rigidity
        • Preferred when heavy product shearing is unavoidable
        • when squealing tends to occur as a consequence of product build-up on the internal surface of the housing.
  • Typically - 8 pockets - minimum of 4 vanes sealing at any one time.
  • More vanes increase the sealing capability but reduce filling efficiency.
  • Replaceable Blades
    • Adjustable on assembly
    • Economic way of introducing alternative MOC that may be required to satisfy the duty:
      • Hardened Steel / Alloy Steel / Hard faced - for abrasion resistance
    • Scraper - To control product build-up on the Housing bore & End Cover faces when necessary
    • Flexible - (usually PU or Natural Rubber) for fibrous or products containing large non friable particles
    • Plastic - Non-stick
    • Bronze or bronze tipped - For ultra close clearances
  • To resist sticky material to hang-up:
    • Rotor with Scalloped pockets
    • Non-stick coating - PTFE, Zylon
    • Manufacturing the Rotor in Stainless Steel & polishing the surfaces
    • Extreme cases - Air purging laterally
  • Helical Vanes / Staggered pockets:
    • To smooth-out the discharge from the valve when necessary
    • Standard rotor will discharge produc within 10% of pocket exposure - pulsed output - where its is problem - use this config
    • Only for Fixed Vane Rotors
