🔩 What is a Coupling?

A coupling is a mechanical element that connects two shafts to transmit rotary motion and torque. Unlike gears or belts, couplings are permanent connections (without intentional slip) and must accommodate shaft misalignment arising from manufacturing tolerances, thermal expansion, and settling under load.

  • Primary function: torque transmission
  • Secondary: misalignment accommodation
  • Tertiary: vibration damping, overload protection, electrical isolation

📐 Types of Misalignment

  • Angular: Shaft centerlines intersect at an angle. Common cause: soft foot, frame distortion.
  • Parallel / Lateral: Shaft centerlines are parallel but offset. Hardest to eliminate by alignment.
  • Axial: Relative axial movement (thermal growth). Critical for pumps connected to hot pipes.
  • Skew / Compound: Combination of all. Real-world condition.
Angular misalignment: θ [°]  |  Parallel offset: δ [mm]  |  Axial: Δx [mm]

⚙️ Key Design Parameters

  • Rated Torque: Continuous duty torque = (P × 9550) / n [N·m]
  • Design Torque: Rated × service factor × app factor
  • Peak Torque: Startup / jam load — check against max coupling rating
  • Torsional Stiffness (K_T): Angular wind-up per N·m — critical for servo drives
  • Lateral Stiffness: Reaction force per mm offset — loads bearings
  • Damping Factor: Flexible elements dissipate vibration energy (elastomers)

🌡️ Thermal & Environmental

  • Elastomers degrade above 90–120°C (NBR), 150–180°C (Hytrel/polyurethane)
  • Gear couplings need oil lubrication — sealed type for clean environments
  • Disc couplings: all-metal, no lubrication, suit high temp and clean rooms
  • ATEX zones: use non-sparking materials, avoid metal-on-metal contact
  • Corrosive environments: SS disc, glass-fibre reinforced spiders

🏗️ Installation & Alignment Best Practices

  • Always align shafts before coupling — coupling is NOT an alignment device
  • Soft foot check before alignment: all feet flat within 0.05 mm
  • Dial indicator / laser alignment to ≤0.05 mm TIR parallel, ≤0.05°/100 mm angular
  • Account for thermal growth (DBSE — Distance Between Shaft Ends) for disc/gear
  • Torque hub bolts to spec — over-torquing splits hubs; under-torquing causes fretting
  • Check flex element at every major maintenance (cracks, hardening, set)
  • Balance coupling if > 1500 rpm — especially grid and gear types
