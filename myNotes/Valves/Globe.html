Globe Valve

Function Globe valves excel in throttling and flow regulation, as well as on/off control, due to their linear motion design.
Design Features a spherical body with a movable disc (plug) that seats against a stationary ring, creating an S-shaped flow path. Stem movement is linear, often manually operated or actuator-driven.
Types
  • Tee Pattern (Z-body): Standard for throttling, with higher pressure drop.
  • Angle Pattern: 90Â° flow redirection, used in piping elbows.
  • Y-Pattern: Reduces pressure drop for high-flow applications.
Applications
  • Flow regulation in refineries (e.g., mixing and separation processes)
  • Steam systems, chemical plants, and HVAC for precise control.
  • Breweries for regulating liquid and gas flow.
Advantages
  • Precise flow control.
  • Good shutoff capability.
  • Shorter stroke than gate valves for faster operation.
Disadvantages
  • Higher pressure drop due to flow path.
  • Not ideal for slurries or viscous fluids.
  • Limited to moderate pressures (up to 300 PSI in some cases)
Materials Stainless steel, cast iron, or brass, with seats often made of PTFE or metal for sealing.
