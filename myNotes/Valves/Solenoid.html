Function Solenoid valves provide precise on/off control in automated systems using electromagnetic actuation.
Design Uses a solenoid to move a plunger or diaphragm, opening/closing the valve. Compact with electrical control.
Types
  • Direct-Acting: Solenoid directly controls the valve.
  • Pilot-Operated: Uses system pressure for operation, suitable for larger systems.
  • Two-Way, Three-Way, or Four-Way: Based on port configuration.
Applications
  • Automation, irrigation, and refrigeration systems.
  • Medical devices and hospital bed air bladders.
  • Breweries for gas and liquid control.
Advantages
  • Fast and precise operation.
  • Ideal for remote and automated control.
  • Compact and lightweight.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to small sizes and low-pressure systems.
  • Requires electrical power.
  • Susceptible to contamination.
Materials Brass, stainless steel, or plastic, with elastomeric seals.
