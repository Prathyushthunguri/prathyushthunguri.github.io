Valves Guide

Key Considerations for Valve Selection

S.No Property Selection
1 Fluid Type Corrosive fluids require materials like stainless steel or PTFE (e.g., diaphragm, plug valves). Slurries favor pinch or diaphragm valves.
2 Pressure and Temperature High-pressure systems need robust valves like gate or trunnion ball valves; high temperatures limit diaphragm and pinch valves.
3 Flow Requirements Full flow (ball, gate) vs. precise throttling (globe, needle).
4 Actuation Manual, pneumatic, electric, or hydraulic, with control valves often requiring automation.
5 Size and Space Butterfly valves are ideal for large pipelines; needle and solenoid valves suit compact systems.
6 Maintenance Diaphragm and pinch valves are low-maintenance; lubricated plug valves require regular upkeep.
7 Cost Butterfly and pinch valves are cost-effective for large sizes; control valves are expensive due to automation.

Standards & Specifications

Standard Specification
API 6D: Pipeline valves (gate, ball).
ASME B16.34: Pressure/temperature ratings for valves.
ANSI/ASME B16.10: Face-to-face dimensions, though diaphragm valves often follow MSS SP-88.
FDA 177:2600: For hygienic valves in food and pharmaceutical industries.
