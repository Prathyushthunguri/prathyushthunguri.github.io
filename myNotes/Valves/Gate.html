Gate Valve

Function Gate valves are designed for on/off control, providing a straight-line flow with minimal pressure drop when fully open. They are not suitable for throttling due to potential damage to the gate and seat.
Design Features a gate (wedge or parallel) that moves perpendicular to the flow path, actuated by a threaded stem connected to a handwheel or actuator
Types
  • Rising Stem: The stem moves visibly up/down, indicating valve position.
  • Non-Rising Stem: The stem remains stationary, ideal for compact spaces.
  • Wedge Gate: Common for tight sealing in liquid systems.
  • Parallel Slide Gate: Used in high-pressure steam or gas systems.
Applications
  • Isolation in oil and gas pipelines, water treatment, and power plants.
  • Used in refineries for crude oil and product isolation.
  • Common in mining and wastewater treatment for handling water and inert substances.
Advantages
  • Common in mining and wastewater treatment for handling water and inert substances.
  • Reliable shutoff for high-pressure and high-temperature systems.
  • Lightweight and simple to operate
Disadvantages
  • Slow operation (multi-turn).
  • Prone to erosion if used for throttling.
  • Susceptible to sediment buildup, affecting sealing.
Materials Carbon steel, stainless steel, or ductile iron, with graphite gaskets for high-temperature applications
