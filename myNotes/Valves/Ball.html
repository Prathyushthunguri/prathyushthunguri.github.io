Ball Valves

Function Ball valves provide quick on/off control with a 90Â° turn, offering excellent sealing for high-pressure applications.
Design Features a spherical ball with a bore that rotates to align with or block the flow. Available with manual, pneumatic, or electric actuation.
Types
  • Full Port: Bore matches pipe diameter, minimizing pressure drop.
  • Reduced Port: Smaller bore, cost-effective but with some restriction.
  • Floating Ball: Relies on fluid pressure for sealing.
  • Trunnion-Mounted: Fixed ball for high-pressure systems.
Applications
  • Petrochemical and natural gas distribution.
  • Pharmaceutical and food processing for hygienic shutoff
  • HVAC and compressed air systems.
Advantages
  • Low friction loss and quick operation
  • Excellent sealing with low torque.
  • Durable for high-pressure applications.
Disadvantages
  • Not suitable for throttling (seat wear).
  • Fluid trapping in the ball cavity when closed.
  • Higher cost for large sizes.
Materials Stainless steel, nickel-plated brass, or UPVC, with PTFE seats.
