Function Plug valves provide on/off and limited throttling control with a quarter-turn operation, similar to ball valves.
Design Features a cylindrical or tapered plug with a bore that rotates to control flow. Compact and robust design.
Types
  • Lubricated Plug: Uses lubricant t reduce friction and enhance sealing.
  • Non-Lubricated Plug: Employs a sleeve (e.g., PTFE) for sealing, reducing maintenance.
Applications
  • Crude oil distillation and product separation in refineries.
  • Chemical processing and slurry systems.
Advantages
  • Quick operation and tight shutoff.
  • Suitable for corrosive and abrasive fluids.
  • Simple design with minimal parts.
Disadvantages
  • Lubricated types require regular maintenance.
  • Higher operating torque than ball valves.
  • Limited throttling capability.
Materials Cast iron, stainless steel, or PTFE-lined for corrosive fluids.
