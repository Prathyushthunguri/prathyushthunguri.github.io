Function Pinch valves control flow by pinching a flexible tube, ideal for slurries and abrasive fluids.
Design Features a flexible sleeve pinched by air pressure or mechanical actuation. Acts as a built-in actuator, reducing the need for external mechanisms.
Types
  • Air-Operated: Uses pneumatic pressure to pinch the sleeve.
  • Mechanical: Uses a handwheel or actuator.
Applications
  • Mining and mineral processing for slurry transport.
  • Wastewater treatment and food processing.
  • Pharmaceutical industry for bulk material handling.
Advantages
  • Handles abrasive and corrosive fluids effectively.
  • No contact between mechanism and fluid, reducing contamination.
  • Cost-effective and low maintenance.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to low-pressure applications.
  • Sleeve wear requires periodic replacement.
  • Not suitable for high-temperature fluids.
Materials Aluminum or stainless steel body; rubber or elastomer sleeve.
