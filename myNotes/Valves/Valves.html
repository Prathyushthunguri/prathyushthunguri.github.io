Valves — Engineering Reference

Key Considerations for Valve Selection

S.NoPropertySelection Guidance
1Fluid TypeCorrosive fluids require materials like stainless steel or PTFE (e.g., diaphragm, plug valves). Slurries favour pinch or diaphragm valves.
2Pressure and TemperatureHigh-pressure systems need robust valves like gate or trunnion ball valves; high temperatures limit diaphragm and pinch valves.
3Flow RequirementsFull flow (ball, gate) vs. precise throttling (globe, needle).
4ActuationManual, pneumatic, electric, or hydraulic, with control valves often requiring automation.
5Size and SpaceButterfly valves are ideal for large pipelines; needle and solenoid valves suit compact systems.
6MaintenanceDiaphragm and pinch valves are low-maintenance; lubricated plug valves require regular upkeep.
7CostButterfly and pinch valves are cost-effective for large sizes; control valves are expensive due to automation.

Gate Valve

FunctionDesigned for on/off control, providing straight-line flow with minimal pressure drop when fully open. Not suitable for throttling due to potential damage to the gate and seat.
DesignFeatures a gate (wedge or parallel) that moves perpendicular to the flow path, actuated by a threaded stem connected to a handwheel or actuator.
Types
  • Rising Stem: Stem moves visibly up/down, indicating valve position.
  • Non-Rising Stem: Stem remains stationary, ideal for compact spaces.
  • Wedge Gate: Common for tight sealing in liquid systems.
  • Parallel Slide Gate: Used in high-pressure steam or gas systems.
Applications
  • Isolation in oil and gas pipelines, water treatment, and power plants.
  • Refineries for crude oil and product isolation.
  • Mining and wastewater treatment for water and inert substances.
Advantages
  • Minimal pressure drop when fully open.
  • Reliable shutoff for high-pressure and high-temperature systems.
  • Simple to operate.
Disadvantages
  • Slow operation (multi-turn).
  • Prone to erosion if used for throttling.
  • Susceptible to sediment buildup, affecting sealing.
MaterialsCarbon steel, stainless steel, or ductile iron, with graphite gaskets for high-temperature applications.

Globe Valve

FunctionExcel in throttling and flow regulation, as well as on/off control, due to their linear motion design.
DesignFeatures a spherical body with a movable disc (plug) that seats against a stationary ring, creating an S-shaped flow path. Stem movement is linear, often manually operated or actuator-driven.
Types
  • Tee Pattern (Z-body): Standard for throttling, with higher pressure drop.
  • Angle Pattern: 90° flow redirection, used in piping elbows.
  • Y-Pattern: Reduces pressure drop for high-flow applications.
Applications
  • Flow regulation in refineries (mixing and separation processes).
  • Steam systems, chemical plants, and HVAC for precise control.
  • Breweries for regulating liquid and gas flow.
Advantages
  • Precise flow control.
  • Good shutoff capability.
  • Shorter stroke than gate valves for faster operation.
Disadvantages
  • Higher pressure drop due to flow path.
  • Not ideal for slurries or viscous fluids.
  • Limited to moderate pressures (up to 300 PSI in some cases).
MaterialsStainless steel, cast iron, or brass, with seats often made of PTFE or metal for sealing.

Ball Valve

FunctionProvide quick on/off control with a 90° turn, offering excellent sealing for high-pressure applications.
DesignFeatures a spherical ball with a bore that rotates to align with or block the flow. Available with manual, pneumatic, or electric actuation.
Types
  • Full Port: Bore matches pipe diameter, minimising pressure drop.
  • Reduced Port: Smaller bore, cost-effective but with some restriction.
  • Floating Ball: Relies on fluid pressure for sealing.
  • Trunnion-Mounted: Fixed ball for high-pressure systems.
Applications
  • Petrochemical and natural gas distribution.
  • Pharmaceutical and food processing for hygienic shutoff.
  • HVAC and compressed air systems.
Advantages
  • Low friction loss and quick operation.
  • Excellent sealing with low torque.
  • Durable for high-pressure applications.
Disadvantages
  • Not suitable for throttling (seat wear).
  • Fluid trapping in ball cavity when closed.
  • Higher cost for large sizes.
MaterialsStainless steel, nickel-plated brass, or UPVC, with PTFE seats.

Check Valve

FunctionAllow unidirectional flow and prevent backflow, protecting equipment from reverse flow damage. Also called non-return valves.
DesignOperates automatically using the fluid's pressure.
Types
  • Swing Check: Hinged disc swings to allow flow and closes to prevent backflow.
  • Lift Check: Disc lifts off the seat, used in high-pressure systems.
  • Ball Check: Ball moves to allow/block flow, ideal for wastewater.
  • Piston Check: Piston-style disc for precise operation.
  • Stop Check: Combines check and manual shutoff functions.
Applications
  • Pump and compressor discharge lines in oil and gas.
  • Water treatment, mining, and sewerage systems.
  • Breweries to prevent backflow in contaminated lines.
Advantages
  • Automatic operation without external control.
  • Prevents equipment damage from backflow.
  • Low maintenance.
Disadvantages
  • Potential for water hammer in high-pressure systems.
  • Limited to unidirectional flow.
  • Not suitable for pulsating flows.
MaterialsCast iron, stainless steel, or ductile iron with stainless steel internals.

Butterfly Valve

FunctionUsed for on/off and throttling, known for compact design and low cost.
DesignFeatures a rotating disc mounted on a rod, turning 90° to control flow. Actuated manually, pneumatically, or electrically.
Types
  • Concentric: Disc centred, for low-pressure applications.
  • Double/Triple Offset (Eccentric): Reduces wear and improves sealing for high-pressure systems.
Applications
  • Large-diameter pipelines in water treatment and mining.
  • HVAC systems and brewery equipment for air and water flow.
  • Refineries for fluid isolation and regulation.
Advantages
  • Lightweight and compact.
  • Low pressure drop compared to globe valves.
  • Cost-effective for large sizes.
Disadvantages
  • Disc remains in the flow path, causing some pressure drop.
  • Limited throttling precision.
  • Sealing may degrade in high-pressure systems.
MaterialsDuctile iron, stainless steel, or UPVC, with elastomeric seats (EPDM, Nitrile).

Plug Valve

FunctionProvide on/off and limited throttling control with quarter-turn operation, similar to ball valves.
DesignFeatures a cylindrical or tapered plug with a bore that rotates to control flow. Compact and robust.
Types
  • Lubricated Plug: Uses lubricant to reduce friction and enhance sealing.
  • Non-Lubricated Plug: Employs a sleeve (e.g., PTFE) for sealing, reducing maintenance.
Applications
  • Crude oil distillation and product separation in refineries.
  • Chemical processing and slurry systems.
Advantages
  • Quick operation and tight shutoff.
  • Suitable for corrosive and abrasive fluids.
  • Simple design with minimal parts.
Disadvantages
  • Lubricated types require regular maintenance.
  • Higher operating torque than ball valves.
  • Limited throttling capability.
MaterialsCast iron, stainless steel, or PTFE-lined for corrosive fluids.

Diaphragm Valve

FunctionIdeal for on/off and throttling in systems handling corrosive, abrasive, or hygienic fluids.
DesignUses a flexible diaphragm that presses against a weir or seat to control flow. Actuated manually, pneumatically, or hydraulically.
Types
  • Weir Type: Common for throttling, with a raised weir for sealing.
  • Straight-Through Type: Full bore for slurries, requiring more diaphragm stretch.
Applications
  • Pharmaceutical, food, and beverage industries for hygienic processes.
  • Wastewater treatment and slurry handling in mining.
  • Corrosive chemical systems.
Advantages
  • Leak-proof seals and tight shutoff.
  • Isolates valve components from fluid, preventing contamination.
  • Easy to clean and maintain.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to moderate temperatures (−62°F to 450°F) and pressures (300 PSI).
  • Diaphragm wear requires regular replacement.
  • Higher pressure drop than gate or ball valves.
MaterialsBody of cast iron or stainless steel; diaphragm of rubber, PTFE, or elastomers compliant with FDA standards.

Needle Valve

FunctionProvide precise flow control in low-flow, high-pressure systems, ideal for fine adjustments.
DesignFeatures a slender, tapered needle that fits into a small orifice, controlled by a finely threaded stem. Linear motion with manual operation.
TypesNo distinct sub-types; variants differ primarily in body orientation (straight, angle, multi-turn).
Applications
  • Laboratory equipment, pressure gauges, and flow metering.
  • Petrochemical and biofuel industries for calibration.
  • Hydraulic and fuel systems.
Advantages
  • Exceptional precision in flow regulation.
  • Reliable shutoff with minimal force.
  • Suitable for high-pressure applications.
Disadvantages
  • High pressure drop due to small orifice.
  • Not suitable for high-flow or viscous fluids.
  • Position not visually verifiable.
MaterialsStainless steel or brass for corrosion resistance.

Pressure Relief / Safety Valve

FunctionProtect systems by releasing excess pressure to prevent equipment damage or catastrophic failure.
DesignAutomatically opens at a preset pressure and closes when pressure normalises.
Types
  • Spring-Loaded: Spring holds the valve closed until pressure exceeds the set point.
  • Pilot-Operated: Uses system pressure to assist operation, ideal for high-pressure systems.
  • Thermal Relief: Protects against pressure buildup from thermal expansion.
Applications
  • Boilers, pressure vessels, and pipelines in oil and gas.
  • Chemical plants and refineries for safety.
  • Power generation facilities.
Advantages
  • Automatic protection against overpressure.
  • High reliability and safety.
  • Prevents costly equipment damage.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to pressure relief, not flow control.
  • Requires regular testing to ensure functionality.
  • Potential leakage if not properly seated.
MaterialsStainless steel, carbon steel, or bronze, with resilient seats for sealing.
Note: Pressure relief valves are safety-critical devices and must be sized, tested, and certified per applicable codes (e.g., ASME Section VIII, API 520/521).

Control Valve

FunctionRegulate flow, pressure, temperature, or liquid level in automated systems, often integrated with actuators.
DesignCombines valve types (globe, butterfly, etc.) with actuators (pneumatic, electric, or hydraulic) for precise control. Equipped with positioners and sensors for automation.
Types
  • Linear Motion: Globe or diaphragm for fine throttling.
  • Rotary Motion: Ball or butterfly for quick operation.
Applications
  • Process control in oil and gas, chemicals, and power plants.
  • Water and wastewater treatment for flow regulation.
  • Automated brewery systems.
Advantages
  • Precise control of process parameters.
  • Seamless integration with automation systems.
  • Versatile for various applications.
Disadvantages
  • High cost due to complexity.
  • Requires calibration and maintenance.
  • Sensitive to fluid contaminants.
MaterialsStainless steel, cast iron, or alloy-based materials.

Solenoid Valve

FunctionProvide precise on/off control in automated systems using electromagnetic actuation.
DesignUses a solenoid to move a plunger or diaphragm, opening/closing the valve. Compact with electrical control.
Types
  • Direct-Acting: Solenoid directly controls the valve.
  • Pilot-Operated: Uses system pressure for operation, suitable for larger systems.
  • Two-Way / Three-Way / Four-Way: Based on port configuration.
Applications
  • Automation, irrigation, and refrigeration systems.
  • Medical devices and hospital bed air bladders.
  • Breweries for gas and liquid control.
Advantages
  • Fast and precise operation.
  • Ideal for remote and automated control.
  • Compact and lightweight.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to small sizes and low-pressure systems.
  • Requires electrical power.
  • Susceptible to contamination.
MaterialsBrass, stainless steel, or plastic, with elastomeric seals.

Pinch Valve

FunctionControl flow by pinching a flexible tube, ideal for slurries and abrasive fluids.
DesignFeatures a flexible sleeve pinched by air pressure or mechanical actuation. Acts as a built-in actuator, reducing the need for external mechanisms.
Types
  • Air-Operated: Uses pneumatic pressure to pinch the sleeve.
  • Mechanical: Uses a handwheel or actuator.
Applications
  • Mining and mineral processing for slurry transport.
  • Wastewater treatment and food processing.
  • Pharmaceutical industry for bulk material handling.
Advantages
  • Handles abrasive and corrosive fluids effectively.
  • No contact between mechanism and fluid, reducing contamination.
  • Cost-effective and low maintenance.
Disadvantages
  • Limited to low-pressure applications.
  • Sleeve wear requires periodic replacement.
  • Not suitable for high-temperature fluids.
MaterialsAluminium or stainless steel body; rubber or elastomer sleeve.
