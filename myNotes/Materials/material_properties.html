Casting vs Wrought: Casting grades generally have higher C, Si and Mn limits than wrought equivalents to ensure castability and fluidity. Mechanical properties are typically slightly lower. Always verify chemistry against the casting standard (ASTM A216, A351, A494, A890, IS 1030, IS 6911) rather than the wrought equivalent.
L-grades (e.g. CF3, CF3M, IS 6911 02Cr grades): Low-carbon casting equivalents (C ≤ 0.03%) corresponding to 304L and 316L wrought grades. Preferred for welded castings to prevent sensitization.
IS 216 vs IS 1030: IS 216 covers grey cast iron (flake graphite) in grades FG 150 to FG 400. IS 1030 covers carbon steel castings (Grades 1–4). IS 1865 covers spheroidal graphite (ductile) iron. IS 6911 covers stainless steel castings — directly equivalent to ASTM A351 CF grades. For Indian projects, IS 6911 castings are accepted in lieu of ASTM A351.

Material Selection Notes & Key Considerations

Ferrous Metals

Carbon Steel (A216 WCB / A105): Most widely used; good mechanical strength but poor corrosion resistance. Requires coating or lining for corrosive service. Weldable with E6013/E7018. Subject to general corrosion in water and acid. Max service temp approx 425°C (higher with alloy steel grades).
Cast Iron (Grey / Ductile): Good compressive strength and damping; brittle in tension. Grey CI not suitable for shock loads. Ductile (SG) iron preferred for valves and pumps. Not weldable by conventional methods without preheat.

Stainless Steels

SS 304 / 304L: General-purpose austenitic SS. Good corrosion resistance in mildly corrosive environments. Susceptible to chloride pitting and stress corrosion cracking (SCC). 304L preferred for welding (low carbon prevents sensitization).
SS 316 / 316L: Addition of Mo (2–3%) significantly improves chloride and acid resistance over 304. Standard choice for marine, chemical and food service. 316L mandatory for welded assemblies. Max service temp 870°C (intermittent).
SS 317L: Higher Mo (3–4%) than 316L. Better resistance to pitting in chloride environments and sulfuric/sulfurous acid service. Used where 316L marginally fails.
Duplex 2205 / Super Duplex 2507: Two-phase (austenite + ferrite) microstructure. Approximately twice the yield strength of 316L. Excellent resistance to chloride SCC and pitting. 2507 for aggressive seawater and acid. Avoid temperatures above 280°C (sigma phase embrittlement).

Nickel Alloys

Hastelloy C-276: Outstanding resistance to a wide range of aggressive chemicals including wet chlorine, HCl, H₂SO₄ and oxidising acids. Most versatile nickel alloy for chemical processing. Weld-without-PWHT capability is a key advantage.
Inconel 625: Outstanding fatigue and creep resistance at high temperatures. Excellent resistance to seawater pitting and crevice corrosion. Used for bellows, flexible hoses and high-pressure vessels. Not suitable for strongly reducing acids.
Alloy 20 (Carpenter 20): Specifically designed for sulfuric acid service. Contains Nb to prevent sensitization. Excellent resistance to dilute and intermediate concentrations of H₂SO₄. Also resists phosphoric acid and many organic acids.
Monel 400: Outstanding resistance to HF acid and fluorine compounds. Also resistant to seawater, neutral salts and reducing acids. Should not be used in oxidising environments or in contact with mercuric salts.

Non-Ferrous Metals

Titanium Gr 2: Outstanding corrosion resistance in oxidising acids (HNO₃, chromic acid), seawater and wet chlorine. Poor resistance to HF and reducing acids (HCl, H₂SO₄) unless inhibited. Excellent strength-to-weight ratio. Requires inert atmosphere welding.
Copper / Copper Alloys: Excellent thermal and electrical conductivity. Good resistance to non-oxidising acids and seawater. Avoid ammonia (forms cuprammonium complexes), oxidising acids and nitric acid. Dezincification risk in brasses with <65% Cu.
Aluminium Alloys: Lightweight with good corrosion resistance via natural oxide film. Attacked by strong acids and alkalis. Not suitable for chloride service above 60°C. Excellent for cryogenic service (no ductile-brittle transition).

Polymers & Plastics

PTFE (Polytetrafluoroethylene): Outstanding chemical resistance — resists virtually all chemicals except molten alkali metals and fluorinating agents. Low coefficient of friction. Max service temp 260°C. Used as lining, gaskets, seats, seals and bellows.
PVDF (Polyvinylidene Fluoride): Excellent chemical resistance to halogens, acids and oxidising agents. Better mechanical properties than PTFE at ambient temperatures. Max service temp 135°C. Used in pipes, valves and pump components for ultra-pure and chemical service.
PP (Polypropylene): Good resistance to non-oxidising acids, alkalis and solvents at ambient temperature. Low cost and lightweight. Max service temp 100–110°C. Brittle below 0°C. Widely used for chemical tanks and piping in lower-temperature service.
PEEK (Polyether Ether Ketone): High-performance engineering polymer. Excellent mechanical properties up to 250°C. Resists steam, hot water, most organic solvents and dilute acids. Used for pump bearings, valve seats and high-temperature seals.

Elastomers

EPDM: Excellent resistance to steam, hot water, ozone, UV and polar solvents. Good resistance to alkalis and dilute acids. Poor resistance to petroleum oils and aromatic solvents. Preferred for food and water service (FDA compliant grades).
Viton (FKM): Outstanding resistance to petroleum fluids, fuels, aromatic solvents, oils and many acids. Good high-temperature performance (200°C). Poor resistance to steam, hot water, esters and ketones.
PTFE / Silicone: Silicone has very wide service temperature range (-60°C to +230°C) and is FDA compliant. PTFE as elastomer is chemically inert. Both used extensively in food and pharma sealing applications.

Engineering Ceramics

Al₂O₃ (Alumina 92–99.5%): Excellent hardness (HV 1500–2000), wear and corrosion resistance. Used for seal faces, bearing surfaces, pump wear parts and linings. Brittle — low impact resistance. Max service temp 1600°C.
SiC (Silicon Carbide): Extremely high hardness (~2500 HV) and excellent thermal conductivity. Outstanding wear and chemical resistance including strong acids and alkalis. Used for mechanical seal faces, nozzles and pump sleeves. Brittle.
WC (Tungsten Carbide): Very high hardness (HV 1400–1800) combined with reasonable toughness (vs. ceramics). Used for HVOF spray coatings, nozzles, valve balls and hard-bearing surfaces. Attacked by strong oxidising acids at elevated temperatures.
