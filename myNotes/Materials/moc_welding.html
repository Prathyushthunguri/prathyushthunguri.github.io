Key Engineering Notes

A. Hardox / Wear-plate Service

Never use SS309 electrodes for welding Hardox sleeves in abrasive service. SS309 is a dissimilar-metal buffer electrode, not a wear-resistant consumable. The weld zone becomes the weakest point and wears faster than the surrounding Hardox. LH 108 or equivalent (Stoody 102, EFe-Cr4) must be used.

B. Dissimilar Metal Joints

When joining Carbon Steel to Stainless Steel, E309L is the standard buffer layer electrode. For critical or high-temperature service, ENiCrMo-3 (Inconel 625 filler) provides superior performance and avoids dilution-related cracking.

C. Sensitization Risk

Always use low-carbon grades (316L, 308L, 309L) when welding stainless steels to avoid carbide precipitation and intergranular corrosion. Standard 316 or 308 should be avoided in welded assemblies unless post-weld solution annealing is performed.

D. Preheat & PWHT

WCB castings typically require preheat of 100–150°C for thicknesses above 25 mm. Hard-facing overlays on Hardox require controlled interpass temperatures (max 250°C) to prevent cracking. Always follow SSAB guidelines for Hardox welding procedures.
