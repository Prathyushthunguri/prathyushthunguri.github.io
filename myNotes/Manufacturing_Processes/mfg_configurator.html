DFM — Design for Manufacturability Rules
Universal DFM Principles
  • Minimise number of parts — combine functions where possible
  • Design for ease of fixturing — flat datum surfaces, consistent grip
  • Avoid tight tolerances unless functionally necessary — each IT grade step = 30–50% cost increase
  • Prefer standard hole sizes and thread forms (metric M-series, ISO)
  • Minimise tool changes — group features requiring same setup
  • Avoid deep blind holes — prefer through holes or step drill accessible depth
  • Corner radii in pockets should equal or exceed end mill radius (no sharp internal corners)
  • Draft angles for castings and mouldings — 1° minimum, 2–3° preferred
Casting DFM
  • Uniform wall thickness — avoid hot spots (ratio max:min wall < 3:1)
  • Add fillets at all junctions — R ≥ 3 mm minimum
  • Avoid undercuts — use split line / core if unavoidable
  • Sand cast: min wall 4–6 mm; HPDC: 1.5–3 mm
  • Include machining allowance — 3–5 mm for sand, 0.5–1 mm for HPDC
Machining DFM
  • All features accessible from max 4 setups (5-axis allows 1–2)
  • L/D ratio of boring/drilling < 5:1 for standard, < 8:1 with support
  • Thin wall sections: avoid < 1 mm (aluminium), < 0.5 mm (steel) in milling
  • Tapped hole depth: 1.5× D minimum, 3× D maximum
  • Prefer 45° chamfers over radii for deburring access
Forging DFM
  • Parting line at largest cross-section
  • No sharp re-entrant angles — min fillet R = 3 mm
  • Flash land + gutter in die design
  • Web thickness: min 6 mm for steel closed die
Additive Manufacturing DFM
  • Overhangs >45° need supports — design self-supporting where possible
  • Minimum wall: 0.8 mm SLM, 1.5 mm FDM, 0.3 mm SLA
  • Lattice fill to reduce weight — maintain skin thickness 1.5–2 mm
  • Avoid large solid masses — heat sink and distortion risk in SLM
  • Post-machining required on mating / bearing surfaces
