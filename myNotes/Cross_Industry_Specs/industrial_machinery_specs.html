Industrial Machinery Technical Specifications — Cross-Industry Comparison

  • Side-by-side comparison across Food & Beverage, Pharmaceutical, Chemical, Paints & Coatings and Minerals & Mining
  • Covers MOC, surface finish, mechanical design, equipment types, instrumentation, compliance and maintenance
  • Each column represents one industry; each row is a technical specification parameter

A. Materials of Construction (MOC) & Surface Finish

  • MOC selection driven by process fluid, regulatory requirements and operating environment

B. Mechanical Design & Pressure / Temperature Specifications

  • Design codes and P/T envelopes vary significantly — Chemical plants operate the widest range; Pharma prioritises hygienic design over extreme ratings

C. Process Equipment Types & Selection

  • Equipment selection shaped by fluid characteristics, contamination risk, cleanability and severity of operating environment

D. Instrumentation, Control & Safety Systems

  • Instrumentation governed by fluid compatibility, regulatory requirements and measurement criticality

E. Compliance, Standards & Certifications

  • Pharma carries the most stringent compliance burden; Chemical plants focus on process safety; Minerals and Paints governed primarily by safety and environmental regulations

F. Maintenance, Cleaning & Utility Requirements

  • Pharma requires change-controlled validated maintenance; Food is designed for rapid CIP/SIP turnaround; Minerals requires frequent wear-part replacement
