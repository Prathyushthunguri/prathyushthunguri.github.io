Sound Absorption in Helmholtz Resonator

Helmholtz resonator (HR) is used to study and analyze resonant frequencies of air cavities. Resonator consists of a cavity with an opening, and it is designed to vibrate at specific frequencies when exposed to sound waves.

How HR reduce noice

  • A resonance occurs when sound with frequency close to resonant frequency passes.
  • At resonance HR absorbts acoustic energy from incoming sound wave.
  • Absorbed energy is dissipated through heat converting kinetic energy to thermal.

How viscous boundary layer aid in sound absorption

  • When sound waves impinge on the surface of a HR, a boundary layer is formed due to the interaction between the vibrating fluid (air) and the solid surface.
  • As the sound wave interacts with the resonator's surface, some of its energy is transferred to the boundary layer, leading to frictional losses and heat generation.
  • This process contributes to the overall absorption of acoustic energy.
  • At resonance frequency, the amplitude of vibration is typically higher, leading to increased interaction with the boundary layer and enhanced energy dissipation.
